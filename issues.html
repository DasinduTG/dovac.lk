CONFLICTS OVER ACCESS TO NATURAL RESOURCES
Land Disputes Due to High-Security Zones and Military Camps

The establishment of high-security zones and military camps on crown land and land previously used by civilians in the Northern and Eastern Provinces has led to disputes. ...

These military installations have resulted in the loss of farming land for small farmers, disrupting their agricultural activities and livelihoods. As these zones are often designated for national security, civilians face restricted access to their own land.

Declaration of Forest and Wildlife Reserves

The declaration of forest and wildlife reserves on land that was previously used for activities like chena cultivation (shifting agriculture) and grazing animals has contributed to conflict. ...

Small farmers who relied on these lands for farming, pasture, or the collection of non-timber forest products lost their access to vital resources. This policy, often imposed without proper consultation, has marginalized farming communities.

Land Grabbing by Politically or Militarily Influential Individuals

Land grabbing is a significant issue, where individuals with political or military influence, or those backed by such figures, seize land that originally belonged to smaller, less influential farmers. ...

This often occurs on crown land or land that small farmers have been cultivating for generations. The displaced farmers are left without their primary livelihood source, worsening socio-economic conditions and contributing to tensions.

Loss of Access to Land for Small Farmers Leading to Disrupted Livelihoods and Outmigration

The combined effects of restricted access to land, forced relocation, and loss of farming opportunities have led many small farmers to lose their livelihoods. ...

As a result, there has been an increase in outmigration from rural areas, especially in the former war zones and border regions. People are often compelled to migrate in search of work or better living conditions, resulting in rural depopulation and increased pressure on urban areas.



ENVIRONMENTAL CONFLICT DRIVERS
Human-Wildlife Conflict

As urbanization and agricultural expansion encroach on wildlife habitats, animals like elephants and wild boars often invade farmland, causing damage to crops. ...

Farmers, in turn, attempt to protect their livelihoods, sometimes through violent means. This has led to heightened tensions between farmers, wildlife conservationists, and authorities.

Conflicts Over Pastureland for Livestock

The shrinking of common pasturelands due to environmental pressures or privatization has led to conflicts between livestock owners and farmers. ...

Cattle herders often graze their animals on agricultural land, damaging crops and resulting in disputes with farmers. These tensions are particularly prevalent in regions where land resources are already limited.

Climate Change and Water Scarcity

The expansion of agriculture often leads to illegal logging and deforestation, which degrades the land and causes soil erosion. ...

This exacerbates environmental damage, affecting local communities and agriculture. Farmers’ attempts to expand crop cultivation by clearing land are often met with resistance from environmentalists and conservation groups, resulting in intense conflicts.

MARKET-INDUCED AGRARIAN PROBLEMS
Unstable Crop Prices

Farmers face volatile prices for their produce, especially during harvest seasons. ...

Crop prices often fluctuate drastically, and farmers are left with little control over market conditions. This economic instability forces farmers into debt or leads them to sell their goods at a loss, creating conflicts with middlemen, traders, and sometimes government authorities over fair pricing and market regulation.

Cheap Imports Undermining Local Farmers

State policies that allow the importation of cheaper foreign agricultural products have undercut local farmers. ...

Imported goods often flood the market at the same time as the local harvest, causing prices to plummet. Farmers argue that these imports make it impossible for them to earn a decent living, and protests against these trade policies have become more frequent.

Lack of Storage and Transport Facilities

Inadequate infrastructure for storing and transporting crops forces farmers to sell their products quickly, often at lower prices than they would receive in a well-functioning market. ...

Without proper cold storage or transportation networks, farmers are unable to manage their harvests effectively, leading to financial losses and conflict with the state over neglected infrastructure.

Mounting Farmer Debt and Financial Crisis

Faced with low crop prices and unpredictable markets, many farmers fall into significant debt, often mortgaging their land and assets to cover costs. ...

This cycle of debt has become a major source of agrarian distress, resulting in conflicts between farmers, financial institutions, and local authorities, as farmers seek debt relief or fairer credit conditions.

STATE POLICIES AND AGRARIAN PROBLEMS
Poor Policy Decision-Making

Government policies, particularly those concerning land, agriculture, and trade, have been poorly planned and often implemented without consulting farmers or agricultural experts. ...

This top-down approach has exacerbated agrarian problems, creating frustration among farmers who feel their needs and perspectives are ignored. The fertilizer and agrochemical ban in 2022, for example, led to widespread crop failures and economic hardship.

Ineffective Land Distribution and Resettlement

State policies regarding land distribution and resettlement in post-war zones have been ineffective, often leading to unfair land allocation. ...

These policies are frequently criticized for failing to prioritize the needs of small farmers, exacerbating inequality, and causing displacement. Land issues, particularly in former conflict areas, have fueled tensions between farmers and the government.

Trade and Import Policies

Sri Lanka’s trade policies have been a major source of conflict, particularly when it comes to agricultural imports. ...

Cheap foreign produce is often allowed into the market, undermining local farmers’ ability to compete. Farmers have raised concerns over the lack of coordination between the Ministry of Agriculture and the Ministry of Trade, which leads to policies that harm local agricultural production.

Lack of Consultation with Farmers in Policy Formulation

The lack of consultation with farmers and agricultural experts during the formulation of policies has led to adverse effects on rural livelihoods. ...

As seen with the controversial agrochemical and fertilizer ban, decisions made without input from those directly affected have led to agrarian distress, igniting protests and social unrest.
